==============================================================================
 V10.6.2 ALTERNATOR — HOW TO READ THE CHARTS  (reading guide, 2026-06-05)
==============================================================================
Two chart types:
  1. ENGINEER CHART  (per truck) : V10.6.2_ALT/visualizations/rul_curves/V10.6.2_ALT_<VIN>_rul.png
  2. FLEET RUNWAY    (whole fleet): V10.6.2_ALT/visualizations/fleet_maintenance_board.png

ONE-LINE FRAME (read this first):
  The system tells you WHICH trucks are at risk (strong) and ROUGHLY WHEN the
  fleet wears out (~601 days). It does NOT predict an exact per-truck failure
  date — that is not possible from this data, and the charts are built to make
  that limit visible, not to hide it.

==============================================================================
 FIGURE 1 — ENGINEER CHART (per truck): 3 panels + optional inset
==============================================================================
HEADER LINE (top): VIN | ridge_prob + risk tier (STATIC = whole-life, not a
  countdown) | age | service state | S(age) | est. km | failed/in-service.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PANEL A — FLEET RELIABILITY  S(t)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHOWS : the fleet-wide probability a truck is still alive at age t (Weibull
        survival curve, fit on n=10 failures), with an 80% posterior band.
        - Black dot + dashed line = THIS truck's current age and its S(age).
        - Red "|" ticks on the x-axis = the 10 actual observed failure ages.
        - Dotted line at 601d = empirical median life.
READ  : where the dot sits = how far through the fleet's life this truck is.
        Dot high-left = young/healthy; dot low-right = deep in wear-out.
        Band width = how uncertain the fleet curve is (small n=10).
TAKEAWAY: "Where this truck sits on the population life curve, and how certain
        that curve is."
DON'T : read it as a per-truck failure date. Only the DOT is truck-specific;
        the curve is the whole fleet.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PANEL B — CONDITIONAL RUL DISTRIBUTION  (the centerpiece)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHOWS : the probability distribution of Remaining Useful Life GIVEN the truck
        survived to its current age (a right-skewed "violin").
        - Ticks at p10 / median / p90 (days).
        - Dash-dot line = the DEPLOYED fleet-clock estimate (601 - age).
        - Failed trucks instead show "EVENT OBSERVED, RUL=0" against the grey
          fleet density they were drawn from.
READ  : the WIDTH (80% prediction interval) is the message. Wide = timing is
        uncertain. median = central estimate; p10 = pessimistic (fails early);
        p90 = optimistic (lasts longer). Use the fleet-clock number for actual
        scheduling (it beat the per-truck model in backtest).
TAKEAWAY: "How many days are left AND how uncertain that is. The spread is the
        answer, not a single number."
DON'T : treat the median as a precise countdown — the band is wide on purpose.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PANEL C — VALIDATION  (alpha-lambda: predicted vs actual)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHOWS : out-of-sample accuracy on the 10 failed trucks (rewound to T-270/-180/
        -90 days). Same panel on every chart (fleet validation context).
        - Dashed diagonal = perfect (pred = actual); grey wedge = +/-20% cone.
        - Green squares = simple FLEET-CLOCK baseline.
        - Coloured dots = the per-truck SURVIVAL MODEL (colour = horizon).
        - Black ring = the current truck (if it is a failed truck).
READ  : points ON/inside the cone = accurate; points ABOVE = over-prediction.
        Green (dummy) hugs the diagonal; model dots sit high & scattered and do
        NOT move toward the diagonal as failure nears.
TAKEAWAY: "Measured proof that the per-truck timing model is worse than a simple
        fleet constant (MAE ~142d vs ~50d) — so we deploy the fleet clock."

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PRECURSOR INSET  (appears on only 2 of 25 charts — this is honest, not a bug)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SHOWS : GED2 excitation-fault rate over the final ~40 days, for the only trucks
        with an adversarially-VERIFIED precursor.
        - VIN1_F : real ~21-day lead (a genuine early warning).
        - VIN10_F: storm fires only ~1 day out (no usable lead).
TAKEAWAY: 1 of 10 failures gives a true ~3-week warning; the other 23 charts
        have NO inset = no precursor (bus voltage normal until failure).

==============================================================================
 FIGURE 2 — FLEET MAINTENANCE RUNWAY (whole in-service fleet)
==============================================================================
SHOWS : one horizontal bar per in-service truck, length = age (days since sale),
        sorted TOP = highest classifier risk.
        - Tan band = fleet wear-out window (578-652d); line at 601 = median.
        - Red dot / "●" = HIGH risk;  blue dot / "○" = LOW risk.
        - Right-side tag = replacement status (OVERDUE +Nd / DUE SOON / PLAN).
        - Grey hatched row = STALE / BLIND (telemetry stopped; can't monitor).
READ (5 seconds):
        1. Top row first = today's #1 work order (highest risk).
        2. Dot past the tan band = overdue for inspection/replacement.
        3. Grey row = can't monitor -> fix telematics, fall back to fleet clock.
        4. Red "●" = classifier-flagged high risk.
TAKEAWAYS:
        - Usually ONE HIGH-risk in-service truck (VIN3_NF) = act on it first.
        - Most trucks are already PAST the 601d median: this is population
          wear-out, NOT an expiry date — do not panic-replace the whole fleet.
        - The 3 grey rows (VIN8/9/15_NF) are blind (no recent telemetry).

==============================================================================
 DECISION RECIPE (how to use the two figures together)
==============================================================================
  STEP 1  Open the FLEET RUNWAY -> read top-down for priority. Act on HIGH-risk
          + past-band first; queue telematics fixes for the grey/blind trucks.
  STEP 2  For any flagged truck, open its ENGINEER CHART:
          - Panel A: how far through life it is (dot on S(t)).
          - Panel B: remaining-life BAND in days + the fleet-clock number to
            put on the schedule.
          - Panel C: reminder the date is a fleet estimate, not a prediction.
          - Inset (if present): a genuine short-term precursor -> treat urgent.

==============================================================================
 WHAT THESE CHARTS DELIBERATELY DO NOT CLAIM (honest limits)
==============================================================================
  - No exact per-truck failure date. RUL = fleet clock; irreducible error ~50d.
  - "Risk" (classifier, AUROC 0.927) = WHICH trucks fail, not WHEN.
  - A real degradation precursor exists for only 1/10 trucks; a flat signal is
    genuinely flat (regulated ~28V until failure), not a missing sensor.
  - km / engine-hours are ESTIMATED (speed-integrated, not odometer).
  - Everything rests on n=10 failures -> wide uncertainty everywhere.

==============================================================================
 GLOSSARY
==============================================================================
  S(t)            Survival probability (fraction still running at age t).
  Posterior band  80% credible range of the fleet survival curve.
  Failure rug     "|" ticks marking the 10 actual failure ages.
  Violin (Panel B) Distribution of remaining life; width = uncertainty.
  p10/median/p90  10th / 50th / 90th percentile of remaining-life days.
  Fleet clock     Deployed RUL estimate = 601 - age (days).
  alpha-lambda    Predicted-vs-actual accuracy plot; +/-20% = "within spec".
  Risk tier       GREEN <0.35 | AMBER 0.35-0.55 | RED >=0.55  (ridge_prob).
  State           NORMAL / DEGRADING (GED storm) / LATE (collapse).
  STALE / BLIND   No recent telemetry; truck cannot be monitored.
