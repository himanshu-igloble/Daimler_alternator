ALTERNATOR — CANDIDATE HEURISTICS TO REDUCE LEAD TIME TO FAILURE
Reference iteration: V10.6.2_ALT (honest-baseline RUL)
Author context: built on the 6 raw CAN channels only (CSP, RPM, ANR, GED, VSI, SMA)
Date: 2026-06-12
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
0. WHY THIS DOCUMENT EXISTS  (the problem V10.6.2 left open)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
V10.6.2 proved three uncomfortable facts:

  (a) The frozen V10.5.3 classifier tells you WHICH truck (AUROC 0.927), but it
      is a static whole-life score — it gives NO timing / lead-time channel.
  (b) The per-truck survival RUL does NOT beat a fleet-clock dummy on day
      accuracy (rewound MAE 142d vs 50d). It only produces a wide band.
  (c) The ONLY genuine short-horizon precursor found in all 6 channels is the
      GED=2 excitation-fault storm — and it fires for just 2/10 failures
      (VIN1 ~21d lead, VIN10 ~1d lead), 0/15 false alarms.

So the open question is: can we extract EARLIER, HIGHER-RECALL precursors from
the SAME six raw channels? V10.6.2 mostly mined VSI level/spread + GED=2 counts.
The candidates below target the signal combinations V10.6.2 did NOT exploit —
chiefly LOAD-NORMALISED and REGIME-CONDITIONED voltage behaviour, crank/recovery
dynamics, and compound multi-channel alarms.

Honest constraint to keep in mind throughout: n = 10 failure events. Any new
heuristic must be validated as DISCRIMINATIVE (within-truck change AND outside
the healthy-fleet NF p05-p95 envelope), exactly as the V10.6.2 forensic engine
does, or it is just curve-fitting to 10 trucks.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. ALTERNATOR OUTPUT CURVE  —  dVSI/dRPM regression slope  [HIGHEST PRIORITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, RPM  (engine-on, valid both)
Idea        : A healthy alternator raises supply voltage with engine speed up to
              its regulation ceiling (~28 V). Regress VSI on RPM per day/week
              (or fit VSI vs RPM-bucketed means). Track:
                - slope dVSI/dRPM in the 600-1500 rpm band (charging ramp)
                - regulation ceiling = plateau VSI at RPM > 1500
                - rpm-at-which-VSI-first-reaches-27V (charging onset speed)
Failure logic: Diode/stator/regulator degradation flattens the ramp and lowers
              the ceiling BEFORE the mean voltage visibly sags — the mean is
              propped up by cruise RPM, which is why V10.6.2's vsi_mean was flat
              for 8/10. Conditioning on RPM removes that masking.
Lead-time win: This is the most physically-motivated untried signal; expected to
              surface gradual electrical-degradation modes weeks earlier than a
              raw-mean drop.
Validate    : per-truck slope trend vs own mid-life baseline; discriminative if
              ceiling or slope drops below NF p05.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. LOAD-NORMALISED VOLTAGE RESIDUAL  —  expected-VSI model  [HIGH PRIORITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI ~ f(RPM, ANR, CSP)
Idea        : Build a healthy-fleet reference surface E[VSI | RPM, ANR, CSP]
              (simple binned lookup or robust linear model on NF trucks). For
              each failing truck compute the daily residual = observed VSI -
              expected VSI at its actual operating point. Track residual mean /
              negative-residual fraction over time.
Failure logic: Removes the confound that V10.6.2 flagged — voltage looks normal
              only because the truck happened to be cruising. A truck whose
              voltage is 0.8 V LOW *for its load* is degrading even if its raw
              daily mean sits inside the fleet band.
Lead-time win: Turns the flat-VSI population (8/10) into a ranked residual-drift
              signal; the single most likely route to >2/10 recall.
Validate    : residual-drift slope, change-point on residual series.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. POST-CRANK VOLTAGE RECOVERY DYNAMICS  [HIGH PRIORITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, SMA (SMA 1->0 edge), RPM
Idea        : V10.6.2 used crank_vsi_min (the sag floor) but threw away the
              RECOVERY. For each start event (SMA falling edge) measure:
                - samples/seconds for VSI to climb back to >=27 V
                - recovery slope (V per sample) in the first ~30 s after crank
                - overshoot / settle behaviour
Failure logic: A weakening alternator or its battery interface recovers slowly
              after the crank load is removed; slow recovery is an early,
              load-stressed test the truck performs every single start.
Lead-time win: Every key-on is a free stress test — high event count even on
              trucks that idle little. Good for trucks with few GED events.
Validate    : per-truck recovery-time trend; discriminative vs NF recovery dist.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. REGULATION DUTY CYCLE  —  fraction of engine-on time in the regulation band
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, RPM (engine-on)
Idea        : Define in-regulation = 27.0 <= VSI <= 29.0 V while engine-on.
              Daily metric = fraction of engine-on samples in band. Complements
              V10.6.2's vsi_sag_frac (which only counts < 24 V) by also catching
              over-charge and "never reaches regulation" behaviour.
Failure logic: Regulator faults push voltage out of the band in EITHER direction;
              a one-sided <24 V threshold misses over-voltage/hunting failures.
Lead-time win: Broader fault-mode coverage than the existing sag-only metric.
Validate    : duty-cycle trend; discriminative if band-fraction < NF p05.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. CUMULATIVE UNDER-VOLTAGE DOSE  (stress accumulator, not daily fraction)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, RPM, timestamp
Idea        : Integrate (24 - VSI) over time while engine-on and VSI < 24 V — an
              accumulating "under-voltage dose" (volt-seconds). V10.6.2 used a
              per-day FRACTION, which resets daily and hides slow accumulation.
Failure logic: Battery sulfation / progressive charge starvation is cumulative;
              a monotone dose curve with a rising knee is an earlier, smoother
              precursor than a noisy daily fraction.
Lead-time win: Smooth monotone signal -> earlier, more reliable change-point.
Validate    : knee/elbow detection on the cumulative curve.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. RESTING-VOLTAGE DECAY SLOPE  (battery state-of-charge proxy)  [interplay]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, RPM==0 (engine-off rows)
Idea        : V10.6.2 computed resting_vsi_mean per day but never TRENDED it.
              Fit a slope of engine-off resting voltage across the truck's life,
              and the overnight decay (last reading before key-off vs first
              reading at next key-on).
Failure logic: A failing alternator under-charges; the battery's resting voltage
              and overnight hold both drift down weeks before a hard failure.
              This is the battery<->alternator interplay the dataset can see.
Lead-time win: Slow-moving, low-noise channel; potentially the earliest of all.
Validate    : resting-slope vs NF; discriminative if slope below NF p05.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CRANK-EFFORT TREND  —  starts/engine-hour and crank duration  [interplay]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : SMA (rising edges = sma_starts), RPM, timestamp
Idea        : V10.6.2 computed sma_starts but used it only as duty context.
              Trend (a) cranks per engine-hour and (b) crank duration (run-length
              of SMA==1) over the truck's life.
Failure logic: Rising crank count / longer cranks indicate hard starting — a
              charging/battery problem upstream of an alternator failure (and a
              direct precursor for the SM dataset). V10.6.2 noted crank-duration
              artifacts in SM; here use it as a slow trend, not a point flag.
Lead-time win: Behavioural precursor independent of voltage; adds an orthogonal
              vote to a compound alarm.
Validate    : duration/count trend vs NF envelope.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. FULL GED STATE-TRANSITION MONITORING  (not just GED==2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : GED (states 0/1/2/3)
Idea        : V10.6.2's emergency layer counts only GED==2 ("disturbance").
              Also trend GED==1 ("excitation not allowed") and GED==3 ("signal
              unavailable") daily rates, and GED state-transition frequency
              (0->2, 0->3 churn).
Failure logic: A rising rate of "not allowed"/"unavailable" can precede the full
              GED=2 storm; transition churn is an instability signature even when
              the absolute GED=2 count stays under the 200/day fire threshold.
Lead-time win: Could extend the GED channel's recall beyond the current 2/10 and
              push VIN1's 21-day lead earlier.
Validate    : per-state daily-rate trend; discriminative vs NF.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. IDLE-VOLTAGE INSTABILITY / REGULATOR HUNTING  (within-regime variance trend)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, RPM (idle band 550-950 rpm), CSP<5
Idea        : V10.6.2 used idle_vsi_mean (level). Instead trend the within-idle
              VARIANCE, lag-1 autocorrelation, and zero-crossing rate of VSI at
              idle (regulator hunting / ripple signature). Optionally a cheap
              FFT/PSD peak on the idle VSI series.
Failure logic: Diode-ripple and regulator hunting raise high-frequency voltage
              variance at a FIXED operating point long before the mean moves —
              and idle is the cleanest fixed point in the data.
Lead-time win: Targets the abrupt/electrical modes V10.6.2 found undetectable
              via level features.
Validate    : idle-variance / autocorr trend vs NF envelope.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. LOAD-INDUCED SAG SEPARATION  (idle-sag vs high-load-sag)  [fault typing]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : VSI, ANR (torque), RPM
Idea        : Split under-voltage events by load: sag while ANR is HIGH (load-
              induced droop) vs sag at idle / low ANR (regulation fault). Track
              the two fractions separately.
Failure logic: High-load sag implicates alternator current capacity (stator/
              diode); idle sag implicates the regulator. Separating them both
              improves recall AND points at the failure mode -> better repair
              guidance, not just earlier warning.
Lead-time win: Recovers signal that V10.6.2's single sag_frac averaged away.
Validate    : per-mode sag-fraction trend vs NF.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. COMPOUND / VOTING EARLY ALARM  (combine the orthogonal channels)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : all of the above
Idea        : Instead of one threshold, build a per-truck WEAK-VOTE alarm: fire
              "early-watch" when >=2 of {dVSI/dRPM ceiling drop, load-residual
              drift, slow post-crank recovery, resting-voltage decay, GED churn}
              cross their NF p05/p95 boundary in the same window.
Failure logic: V10.6.2 showed no SINGLE channel generalises past 2/10. A 2-of-N
              vote across ORTHOGONAL physics trades a little precision for recall
              and earlier first-trigger, while the existing GED=2 storm stays as
              the separate high-precision "days-left" emergency.
Lead-time win: The realistic path to detecting more than 2/10 with a usable lead.
Validate    : LOVO over the 10 failed VINs; report recall, false-alarm rate on
              the 15 NF, and first-trigger lead distribution — same honest gate
              as V10.6.2 (must beat fleet-clock on first-trigger lead, 0 NF fires
              is the precision target).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. PER-TRUCK CHANGE-POINT DETECTION  (earliest-deviation timestamp)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Raw signals : any engineered series above (best on load-residual, resting-V)
Idea        : Run online CUSUM or Bayesian change-point on each truck's OWN
              normalised feature series; the change-point timestamp IS the lead
              time. Removes dependence on a fleet threshold entirely.
Failure logic: Each truck is its own control; the question "when did THIS truck
              depart from ITS baseline" is exactly the lead-time question, and
              avoids the fleet-envelope dilution that hides slow individual drift.
Lead-time win: Directly maximises lead time by definition; pairs naturally with
              #2 and #6.
Validate    : change-point lead vs JCOPENDATE; require detection before T-7d on
              more than the current 2/10 to count as progress.

PRIORITISATION (given the V10.6.2 evidence)
================================================================================
  Tier 1 (most likely to add lead time):  #1 dVSI/dRPM,  #2 load-residual,
                                           #3 post-crank recovery,  #11 voting
  Tier 2 (slow, low-noise early channels): #5 UV dose,  #6 resting decay,
                                           #12 change-point
  Tier 3 (fault-mode coverage / typing):   #4 duty cycle, #8 GED states,
                                           #9 idle hunting, #10 sag typing,
                                           #7 crank effort

WHAT NOT TO EXPECT
  - None of these will turn the per-truck survival RUL into a precise date; the
    fleet-clock limit (V10.6.2 sec 5) is structural at n=10.
  - The honest deliverable shape stays: WHICH (classifier) + WHEN-fleet (Weibull
    window) + WHEN-emergency (precursor alarm). These candidates aim to make the
    third box fire EARLIER and for MORE trucks, not to manufacture daily RUL.

VALIDATION HARNESS TO REUSE
  V10.6.2_ALT/src/V10.6.2_ALT_forensic_features.py already implements the exact
  honest test (within-truck z>=2 AND outside NF p05-p95, MIN_EO_SAMPLES=200 trust
  filter, 90/60/45/30/14/7d horizon bins). Add each new feature to its feat_cols
  / daily panel and read earliest_signal_per_vin.csv to see if recall rises above
  the current 2/10 baseline.
