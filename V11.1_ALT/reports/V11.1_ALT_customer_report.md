## 1. Executive Summary

**What V11.1 asked:** Can covariate signals derived from the V11 heuristic channels (x1 = log-lifetime GED+compound exceedance count; x2 = trailing compound-vote indicator) allow an Accelerated Failure Time (AFT) model to deliver per-truck RUL estimates that beat the fleet-clock dummy?

**The honest answer: No.** This is the **third structural confirmation** that individualized RUL is not achievable from these 6 CAN channels at n = 25 trucks.

**Evidence:**
- Chosen variant M0 (baseline, no covariates): MAE 140.4d vs fleet-clock dummy 49.7d.
- M1 (x1 lifetime exceedance count): MAE 148.8d — worse than M0.
- M2 (x1 + x2 trailing vote): MAE 162.2d — worst of three.
- All Wilcoxon signed-rank tests vs dummy: p = 1.0 (no variant improves).
- The x1 covariate is **exposure-confounded**: non-failed trucks accumulate more lifetime exceedances simply because they live longer (the signal tracks age, not pathology). See §2.

**What ships:**
1. **Frozen classifier** (V10.5.3, AUROC 0.927) — risk ranking of the 15 in-service trucks.
2. **M0 fleet curve** ≡ V10.6.2 Weibull (shape 5.17, scale 771d) — fleet replacement window anchored at **601d empirical** (survival median 718d, context only).
3. **GED emergency channel** — GED=2 storm fires for **2/10** failed trucks, **0/15** NF false alarms.
4. **Current-state early-watch** (compound-only, deployable) — fires for **3/10** currently-active failed trucks, **0/15** NF false alarms.
5. Ever-fired exceed and compound channels are **report-only** (NF false-alarm rates: 2/15 exceed, 6/15 compound).

### 2b. Why x1 failed — the exposure confound

x1 = log(lifetime count of days where GED=2 or compound-vote ≥ 2) was intended to capture electrical stress history. However, non-failed trucks **accumulate more lifetime exceedances because they live longer**: the signal is a proxy for age, not pathology. Any marginal AFT coefficient is therefore absorbing the same timing information already in the Weibull baseline — it cannot differentiate early vs late failures independent of exposure.

A time-varying covariate (exceedances per unit time) would be needed to break the confound, but requires a substantially larger event set to estimate reliably.

### 2c. G-BETA gate

G-BETA criterion: chosen variant must either (a) have Wilcoxon signed-rank p < 0.05 vs dummy, or (b) reduce mean PI-width by ≥ 15% relative to M0. Neither M1 nor M2 satisfies either criterion.

**Gate G-BETA: PASS — verdict: NO_IMPROVEMENT_HONEST**

## 9. Limitations

1. **n = 10 failure events.** All survival quantities carry wide uncertainty. The fleet Weibull shape and the AFT beta coefficients are under-identified; empirical medians are more reliable point estimates than model medians.
2. **Exposure confound (x1).** Lifetime exceedance count is systematically higher for non-failed trucks (they accumulate more time). A rate-based time-varying covariate would require n ≫ 10 events to estimate.
3. **Aged fleet — time dimension non-discriminating.** All 15 in-service trucks carry time_dim = SHORT because the fleet is old. Remaining-life estimates overlap heavily; the time dimension adds no actionable separation beyond the fleet schedule.
4. **No per-truck failure dates.** No calendar timestamps are available; TTF is measured in operational days from first record. km / engine-hours are estimated (speed-integrated, not odometer).
5. **Ever-fired multiple comparisons.** Testing 12+ heuristics against 10 failures inflates the chance of spurious hits. The current-state early-watch channel is the conservatively validated subset (0/15 NF false alarms at current state).
6. **Classifier frozen.** ridge_prob is a static whole-life score (V10.5.3). No retraining; its risk discrimination is borrowed by the RUL layer for ranking, not timing.
