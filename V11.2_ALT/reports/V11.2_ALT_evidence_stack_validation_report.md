## 2. RUL end-of-life & continuity analysis (Tasks 3, 4, 5)

**Finding: the fleet clock over-predicts remaining life for every failed truck — by 112–268 days.**
Computed directly from `vin_lifecycle` + the conditional Weibull + `JCOPENDATE`:

| VIN | Age @ last | Schedule RUL @ last | Actual days to failure | **Over-predicted** | Clock zone @ failure | Condition (M5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| VIN1 | 596 | 173 d | 11 d | **162 d** | YELLOW | RED |
| VIN2 | 627 | 158 d | 0 d | 158 d | YELLOW | ORANGE |
| VIN3 | 591 | 178 d | 66 d | 112 d | YELLOW | GREEN |
| VIN4 | 664 | 133 d | 0 d | 133 d | YELLOW | YELLOW |
| VIN5 | 661 | 135 d | 0 d | 135 d | YELLOW | GREEN |
| VIN6 | 552 | 205 d | 0 d | 205 d | **GREEN** | GREEN |
| VIN7 | 673 | 130 d | 0 d | 130 d | YELLOW | GREEN |
| VIN8 | 573 | 189 d | 0 d | 189 d | **GREEN** | YELLOW |
| VIN9 | 606 | 169 d | 2 d | 167 d | YELLOW | GREEN |
| VIN10 | 472 | 268 d | 0 d | **268 d** | **GREEN** | ORANGE |

**Answers to the review questions:**
1. *Why does large RUL remain right before failure?* The RUL line is the **age-based fleet survival
   clock**; at ~600 d age (just under the 601 d fleet median) the Weibull posterior still assigns
   ~130–270 d median remaining. It has **no per-truck input** — it cannot see an individual truck failing.
2. *Is the survival model over-estimating?* **Yes — systematically and by design**, for these premature
   failures. This is the honest limitation, not a bug; it is the visual proof of the project's core
   finding ("per-truck RUL cannot beat the fleet clock").
3. *Is the failure date forcing an artificial collapse?* The JCOPENDATE clip correctly sets RUL = 0 at
   the **true** failure. The drop's *steepness* is the over-prediction (130–270 d of schedule meeting a
   premature failure). For 7/10 trucks the last reading **is** the failure date, so the drop is instantaneous.
4. *Should RUL decline smoothly to failure?* **No.** A smooth per-truck decline would require a per-truck
   physics-RUL that **three iterations proved does not exist** (MAE ~140 d vs ~50 d fleet clock).
   Smoothing would fabricate a decline that never happened.

**Continuity verdict:** large vertical drops here are **justified** — they encode genuine premature
failure, not a plotting artifact. The only artifact was the *un-labelled* straight interpolation line,
now replaced.

## 3. Zone consistency: Section 1 vs Section 1b (Task 2)

**These two zone systems intentionally measure different things and must be allowed to diverge:**

| | Panel 1 — Schedule zones | Panel 1b — Condition zones (M5) |
|---|---|---|
| Driven by | **Age** (fleet survival clock) | **This truck's signals** (VSI deviation/volatility, GED2 rate, VSI range) |
| Answers | "Where is this truck on the fleet's wear-out *schedule*?" | "What is this truck's measured *condition* right now?" |
| Thresholds | RUL days 180/90/30 | M5 score 0.15/0.35/0.55 |

Synchronising them would require **either** a per-truck physics-RUL (proven unachievable) **or** hiding
the divergence — and the divergence is the **value**. For **VIN1** the condition hit **RED** while the
schedule still read **YELLOW**: the condition signals caught what the schedule could not. Across the
fleet, condition is *more* alarmed than schedule for VIN1/VIN10 (the actionable cases) and equally quiet
for the silent failures (VIN5/VIN7) — exactly the honest picture.

**Correction applied:** Panel 1 retitled **"FLEET SCHEDULE (age-based, not per-truck)"**, Panel 1b
**"CONDITION — this truck's own signals"**, with a footer stating they answer different questions and
diverge when a truck fails off-schedule. No threshold or logic change.

## 4. VIN1 detailed case study (Tasks 3, 8, 9)

`VIN1_F_ALT_evidence_stack.png` — the benchmark management graph.

- **Timeline:** sale-referenced start 2024-04-01; last telemetry 2025-11-18 (age 596 d); **JCOPENDATE
  2025-11-29**. ~135k km, ~5,328 engine-hours at failure.
- **Schedule (Panel 1):** fleet clock showed **~173 d remaining** at last contact (zone **YELLOW**).
  Truck failed **11 d later** → **over-predicted by ~162 d**. Now labelled explicitly on the graph.
- **Condition (Panel 1b):** M5 health-zone climbed **GREEN → YELLOW → ORANGE → RED** (driver: VSI
  volatility); ends **RED** — the condition view *did* flag danger the schedule missed.
- **Precursor (Panel 3):** the **GED2 excitation storm fired ~21 d before failure** (validated emergency
  channel — VIN1 is one of only 2/10 trucks with this signature). The earlier "resting-voltage ~120 d"
  claim was an early-life artifact and has been removed.
- **Voltage (Panel 2):** persistent late sag activity consistent with regulation stress.
- **Charging (Panel 4):** V–RPM ceiling essentially stable (this is an excitation-fault mode, not a
  charging-ceiling collapse).

**Management reading of VIN1:** "The fleet *schedule* said this truck was mid-life (YELLOW, ~173 d), but
its *own electrical condition* went RED and the excitation-fault pager fired ~21 days out — and it failed
~162 days ahead of the schedule. **This is the case for acting on condition + the fleet window, not a
per-truck day-count.**" That is a clear, honest, defensible story.

## 7. Honest limitations (carried into every figure)
- Per-truck RUL point estimates do not beat the fleet clock; the **band + 601 d / ~120k km window** are
  the timing deliverables.
- Genuine at-failure precursors exist for only **4/10** failed trucks (VIN1 GED 21 d, VIN2 ~16 d,
  VIN6 ~11 d, VIN10 ~11 d/GED 1 d); **6/10 are silent** — abrupt failures with no electrical warning.
- The M5 health-zone is **supporting context** (weak sensitivity; can peak transiently — now labelled).
- All quantities carry the **n = 25 / 10-event** data ceiling.

## 8. Updated graph-generation guidelines (Task 9, deliverable 9)
1. Never interpolate a smooth per-truck RUL decline; end the schedule at last observation and mark the
   actual failure separately, **labelling the over-prediction**.
2. Keep schedule (age) and condition (physics) as **distinct** panels with distinct labels; never sync.
3. A precursor "fires" only if **elevated at failure** (z ≥ 2 at the end); lead = onset of that final run.
4. M5 transitions are reported only when **sustained to the end**; transient peaks are labelled.
5. GED2 storm comes from the **authoritative emergency channel**, never a weekly-burst proxy.
