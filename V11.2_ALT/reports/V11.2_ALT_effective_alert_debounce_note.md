# Why Section 0 flickered GREEN→YELLOW→GREEN, and the debounce that fixes it

**Audience:** DICV reviewers and engineers reading the per-VIN evidence stacks
(`rul_evidence_stack_effective/`, `rul_evidence_stack_snapshot/`).
**Scope:** the top strip only (Section 0). Panels 1, 1b, 2–4 are unchanged.

---

## 1. The observation

On some trucks the top **EFFECTIVE ALERT** strip switched GREEN→YELLOW→GREEN several
times across the life. VIN3_F is the extreme case: **~11 flips, 23 colour runs.**

## 2. Why it is possible (the mechanism)

Section 0 paints `max(schedule_level, condition_level)` at **daily** resolution.

- **The schedule cannot cause it.** The schedule RUL is a fleet-clock median forced
  monotone (`np.minimum.accumulate`). Verified across **all 25 trucks**: the schedule
  level never decreases on any day. An age-clock is a one-way ratchet
  (GREEN→YELLOW→ORANGE→BLACK), so it can never send a zone *back* to green.
- **The condition does.** The M5 health score is a continuous daily signal that moves
  **up and down**. For VIN3 it sits right on the 0.15 GREEN/YELLOW line all year:

  | VIN3 M5 over life | value |
  |---|---|
  | min / mean / max | 0.108 / 0.145 / 0.321 |
  | days within ±0.02 of 0.15 (of 566) | **335 (59%)** |

  Day-to-day noise nudges M5 just above 0.15 (→YELLOW) then just below (→GREEN), again
  and again. Every upward flip in the raw trace is driver=`condition`; only the final
  flip is driver=`schedule` (age finally crossing 180 d at end-of-life).

**So the flicker is real boundary-chatter on a continuous score near a hard cut-point —
not a bug, and not invented. But most of it is noise, not health change.** Only one VIN3
excursion is a genuine swing: the **21-day June-2024 bump to M5≈0.32.**

### Section 1 vs Section 1b — two different zone engines

| | Section 1 — schedule | Section 1b — condition (M5) |
|---|---|---|
| Input | age (days lived) | physics: VSI deviation/volatility, GED2 rate, range |
| Switches on | RUL crossing 180/90/30 **days** | M5 crossing 0.15/0.35/0.55 **score** |
| Direction | one-way ratchet (monotone) | up **and** down (oscillates) |
| VIN3 | 1 shift (GREEN→YELLOW, end-of-life) | line crosses 0.15 ~11×, **0 sustained** |

Panel 1b looks calmer than the raw strip only because it **labels** sustained-to-end
transitions (noise-free), while the raw strip **coloured** every daily crossing. Same M5,
two rendering rules.

## 3. The fix — an "act-on" rule on the condition only

Section 0's condition now escalates only when **both** hold:

1. **Entry deadband** — M5 must CLEAR a zone boundary by **+0.02**
   (YELLOW at 0.17, ORANGE at 0.37, RED at 0.57). Removes the shallow wobbles that sit
   0.000–0.012 above 0.15 — within M5 measurement noise.
2. **Dwell** — the new zone must HOLD for **≥14 consecutive days.** Removes short spikes.

The **schedule is left raw** (it is monotone and never chatters), so a real age-escalation
is never hidden — even a short one at end-of-life. **Panel 1b keeps the raw 0.15/0.35/0.55
zones** for diagnostics; only the act-on layer is deliberately less twitchy (standard ops
practice: alert thresholds sit above diagnostic thresholds to prevent alert fatigue).

## 4. Effect (honest before/after)

| Config | VIN3 alert episodes | Fleet eff-runs (all 25) |
|---|---|---|
| Raw (daily, 0.15) | ~11 flips / 23 runs | 191 |
| Dwell only (≥14 d) | 7 | 118 |
| **Dwell + 0.02 deadband (shipped)** | **2** | **100** |

VIN3's honest floor is **2**, not 1: one real condition excursion (June, M5 0.32) **plus**
one genuine schedule yellow at end-of-life. Neither is hidden.

## 5. Plain-language summary

VIN3's health score sat on the green/yellow line for most of its life; tiny daily wiggles
tipped it just over and just under, so the act-on strip kept blinking. It was never the
age-clock blinking (that only worsens with time) and nothing was faked — but those blinks
were noise on the line, not real health swings. We now raise the alert only when the score
**clearly** crosses a line **and stays there two weeks**, which turns VIN3's blinking into
its **one** real condition event plus its end-of-life age warning.
